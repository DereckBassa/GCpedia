Service Management at PSPC

Let’s change the way we connect to, manage and deliver services at PSPC.


PSPC is developing a department-wide approach to providing our customers with value in the form of services through the adoption of service management practices. Based on the sum of industry best practices and reference models found in the processes of IT Service Management (ITSM), this enterprise-wide and unified approach to managing service can be applied to any form of service, not just information technology.


PSPC’s Client Service strategy and approach to service management are guided by the Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat (TBS) Policy on Service Principles of client-centric service delivery, increasing operational efficiencies, and promoting a culture of management excellence. They also support the PSPC Transformation Roadmap priority ‘Changing the Way We Deliver Services'.

Key Elements of Service Management at PSPC

  • Integrated Service Management
  • PSPC Enterprise Service Management Office
    • Digital Services Branch SMO
    • Real Property Services SMO

The ISM Vision

All of these elements are currently being developed into an ‘enterprise-wide’ model across PSPC to provide more communication, transparency and responsiveness to client requests for services. This shared approach will be enabled through Integrated Service Management (ISM) to modernize the flow of work through an advanced service management platform.
